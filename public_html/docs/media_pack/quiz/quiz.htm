Quiz

 

Account Executive / District Manager

 

 

 

  1. Who can become a Go DEALER to DEALER member?
    1. Any company who has a vendor’s license
    2. Anybody no matter where they live
    3. Only authorized dealers of Auto, Truck, Rental, Marine, RV, Power Sports, or Motorcycles.
  2. How much does it cost to become a Go DEALER to DEALER member?
    1. There are no membership fees
    2. $100.00 a month
    3. $1,500 setup fee and $100.00 a month
  3. When a member wins an auction, how much is the buy fee?
    1. A flat $300.00
    2. 10% of the winning bid price
    3. A sliding scale set percentage based on the winning bid price
  4. How much is a “Buy Now” fee?
    1. A flat $300.00
    2. The same as a normal buy fee
    3. 5% of the winning bid price
  5. How much does it cost a dealer to SELL an item?
    1. A flat $300.00
    2. A sliding scale set percentage based on the winning bid price
    3. There are no sell fees
  6. What is the fee to PULL an active auction?
    1. A flat $300.00
    2. A sliding scale set percentage based on the winning bid price
    3. There are no pull fees
  7. What 2 types of payment will Go DEALER to DEALER accept for our services?
    1. Check by Phone or PayPal
    2. Auto billing to either Credit Card or Bank Draft
    3. Manual Invoice or Certified Check
  8. How does a winning bidder get notified when they have won an auction?
    1. An automated email and online under Bids for Auctions Won
    2. A phone call from Go DEALER to DEALER technical support
    3. A certified letter from the selling dealer
  9. How does the rating system function?
    1. Go DEALER to DEALER will rate each dealer on how well they are listing their auctions which will reflect on every auction that dealer creates
    2. The buyer rates the seller on 7 different criteria regarding the auction and the overall transaction which will reflect on every auction that dealer creates
    3. The manager at the dealership rates their sales staff on units sold per month which will reflect on every auction that dealer creates
  10. When, and if there are any changes to the User Agreement, how will a member be notified?
    1. A phone call from Go DEALER to DEALER technical support
    2. A notification in the News section with a related article explaining the change and the updates shown in detail in the Help section.
    3. A certified letter from the Account Executive or District Manager
  11. Who is responsible for the information that is listed on Go DEALER to DEALER when creating an auction?
    1. The selling dealer
    2. The bidding dealer
    3. The assigned Account Executive or District Manager
  12. Who makes the contact when an auction is over?
    1. Either the selling or the buying dealer must contact each other within 24 hours of the close of the auction
    2. The buying dealer must call any salesman from the dealership to purchase the item through them
    3. The selling dealer must transport the unit to the closest physical auction for pickup
  13. When a selling dealer’s reserve is met, what does the seller need to do with the item?
    1. Continue to retail the item as soon as possible
    2. Transport the item to the closest physical auction
    3. Remove the item from their retail lot and prepare for sale to another dealer
  14. If a dispute arises under the Go DEALER to DEALER agreement regarding an item between dealers, what important steps need to be taken?
    1. The selling dealer MUST sell the buyer another vehicle from their lot at a price equal or greater to the original.
    2. The 2 parties will consult the Mediation and Arbitration section 10 of the User Agreement and follow the process as documented
    3. The buying dealer must complete the transaction with a “buyer beware” clause.
  15. When are the fees processed?
    1. Daily
    2. Every Friday
    3. The 1st of each month
  16. When should a selling dealer pull an auction?
    1. When they have the item sold retail and has not met the reserve price
    2. As soon as the reserve is met
    3. After the Buy Now option is executed
  17. What is a request auction?
    1. Where a selling dealer posts an auction, all other dealers are emailed
    2. When conducting a search, items that are not in an active auction and is still in the seller’s inventory, the item is available to request for auction
    3. When a buying dealer purchases an item and requests the item to be shipped to their location
  18.  How does the Make Offer option work?
    1. After an auction closes and the reserve is not met, both highest bidder and the seller have the opportunity to make the other party an offer
    2. All auctions below $1,000 have the ability for a bidder to make an offer
    3. Dealers who fall below average rating can no longer make bidding auctions, they now can only create make offer auctions.
  19. What is a reserve price?
    1. The highest acceptable wholesale price the seller is wanting for the item
    2. The lowest acceptable wholesale price the seller is wanting for the item
    3. The average retail price of the item being listed
  20. What documents are needed to process a membership application?
    1. A scan of the credit card, drivers license, and signed affidavit from the owner of the dealership
    2. A check for the $1,500 signup fee, signed application form, and copy of the dealers license or vendors license
    3. Signed application form, and a copy of the dealer’s license or vendors license
