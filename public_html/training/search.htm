Searching

Searching the site for a desired vehicle / item can be accomplished two ways. One is a precise and direct way and the other is more of a “browse” method. The direct way to search is to use the Search Bar. The “browse” method is done by using the Categories. These are described below.

Search Bar

The Search Bar is used to conduct precise searches of the current active auctions in the database. If the vehicle/item is not in the Open Auction mode, it will not be available for search. Auction modes and activating auctions are covered in Creating an Auction.

The Search Bar is located just under the Time and Date section on the Menu Bar and is visible from every screen in the site. See fig 4.1 for an example of the Search Bar with a sample search criteria entered.

You can better define your search by entering a range for the year. We are using the range from 1995 to 2000 for our advanced search. Only items within this time will be displayed.

You can also choose an exact Make and Model and only get those items as well. We will leave this section to ALL for this example.

You can also narrow your results by selecting a specific group of items within a certain set of miles. We will use less than 50K miles for our search.

The State you wish to search can also be selected to narrow your search results to just that State. All other items in other states will not be displayed. If you would like to search multiple states close to your area, you can leave the State field to ALL and select a Region. To see what states are the that Region, there is a US map below the form that is color coded to the Region lists.

Categories

The other method to search the database is by browsing the categories. This example is using a sample database and is not fully published with as many entries as the real database will have.

There are two (2) locations where you can use the Categories search option. The User’s Home Page, as discussed in User’s Home Page, has a Categories section to the left of the screen. This shows all the levels of categories that auctions are listed by clicking on the appropriate link.

The other search location is the Categories Bar. This is located at the middle top area of the main screen and like the Search Bar, is visible from every screen in the site. This is a pull down menu where you can then select the appropriate level of category and click the Browse button to view all active auctions under that category. Fig 4.5 shows a pull down for Passenger Vehicles and all the results of the search.

fig 4.5

This result could show many active auctions under the Passenger Vehicles category. If your result displays many items, only 50 entries will be listed per page. Simply select “NEXT” to proceed to the next page. This method is called browsing the categories. At the bottom of each page is listed the total number of items and the page number you are looking at.
